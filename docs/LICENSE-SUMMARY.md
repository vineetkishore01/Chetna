## Enforcement Strategy

### Good Faith Approach

1. **Education First**: Most violations are unintentional
2. **Reasonable Time**: 30 days to come into compliance
3. **Proportional Response**: Match response to violation severity
4. **Last Resort**: Legal action only for persistent violations

### What to Monitor

- ❌ Competing "Chetna Cloud" services
- ❌ Misleading product names
- ❌ Domain/squatting issues
- ❌ Endorsement claims

### What to Ignore

- ✅ Accidental violations (if corrected)
- ✅ Fair use (reviews, commentary)
- ✅ Non-commercial community content
- ✅ Minor technical violations

## FAQ

### Q: Do I need to register the trademark?

**A**: Not immediately. Common law trademark rights exist from first use. Registration strengthens protection but isn't required for basic enforcement.

### Q: What if someone violates the license?

**A**: Most violations are unintentional. Send a friendly notice citing the license/trademark policy. Most will comply voluntarily.

### Q: Can I change the license later?

**A**: Yes, but you'll need consent from all contributors (since they hold copyright on their contributions). Best to get it right now.

### Q: What about patents?

**A**: MIT doesn't explicitly grant patent rights. If this becomes important, consider adding an explicit patent clause or switching to Apache 2.0.

### Q: Do I need a lawyer?

**A**: For basic protection, these templates should suffice. For commercial licensing or enforcement, consult an IP attorney.
